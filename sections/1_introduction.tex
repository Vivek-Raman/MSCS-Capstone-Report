\section{Introduction}

When researchers draft an academic document like a thesis, chapter, report, or paper, they often use \LaTeX, a typesetting system that is purpose-built to prepare technical and scientific documents \cite{latex}.
\LaTeX\ separates the authors' content from the rules that define the document's formatting.
This allows publishers and editors to maintain or enforce consistency in formatting without affecting the content itself.

While \LaTeX\ makes it easy for editors of publications, it requires authors to learn its syntax and semantics.
To experts and repeat authors, this is not a problem, but for students, this is a difficult ask.

In parallel, large language models (LLMs) and generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) have become powerful productivity tools across many domains.
However, in academic contexts, the adoption of GenAI raises concerns about academic integrity through inadvertent plagiarism or hallucinated citations and claims.

This report introduces Spartan Write: an AI agent for academic documentation.
Spartan Write is designed to provide AI assistance without altering the content.
The key principle guiding Spartan Write is this: \textbf{the tool only works with content that the author provides}.
It focuses entirely on layout, syntax, and formatting.

At its core, Spartan Write pairs an intelligent agent with \LaTeX.
\LaTeX\ guarantees the formatting consistency, while the agent makes edits through natural conversation.
These edits can be to structure tables, place figures and captions, handle LaTeX syntax, or to organize content.
The agent also supports scanning content like tables from a handwritten note and converting it into \LaTeX.
